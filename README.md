# Solar-Powered-Photocatalytic-Hydrogen-Generation-for-Advanced-IC-Engines/Machineries-Applications

Project proposes a PV-E based system that generates hydrogen and oxygen from water using solar energy. Unlike conventional hydrogen systems, the produced oxygen is retained and utilized with hydrogen for controlled high-efficiency combustion, reducing dependence on external air and enabling cleaner, aerospace-grade propulsion and power generation.

**Technical Approach to Hydrogen Fuel Production** 
#
The technical approach to Hydrogen fuel production has been divided into several key methodological areas, from Feasible to Lab Experiments. 

There are several methods like from Extraction from atmosphere to Elemental Separation of Water into Hydrogen and Oxygen by Electrolysis.

* In Electrolysis process the electricity is passed through two metallic rods known as electrodes (Anode and Cathode), on the positive and negative charges. 
* These charges move through water by breaking their bonds to separate them on single elements at the ends of anode and cathode. 
* This Electrolysis process is done by providence of electricity, so how this electricity is formed and from which source depends on the name of the Hydrogen Colour Grading.
* Like from Nuclear Power gives white hydrogen, Coal/Fuel Power gives grey hydrogen, or like if it's coming from some Renewable sources (wind or solar) then it is called as Green Hydrogen.

**1. Current Scenario:**

In conventional Internal Combustion Engines (IC Engines):

*Fuel*
* Petrol
* Diesel
* CNG
* LNG
* Biofuels

*Oxidizer*
* Atmospheric air
Air contains:
~ 21% Oxygen
~ 78% Nitrogen
~ 1% Other gases

*Problem*

Only oxygen supports combustion.

_The nitrogen:_
* Occupies engine volume.
* Absorbs combustion heat.
* Produces NOₓ emissions at high temperatures.
* Reduces combustion efficiency.

_*Thus, a large portion of intake air is essentially "dead weight."*_

Producing:
 * Hydrogen fuel
 * Oxygen oxidizer

* Storing only H₂
